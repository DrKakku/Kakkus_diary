### Some metrics on indias population future

India's population is projected to peak around 1.7 billion in the early 2060s, after which it is expected to decline, though the country will remain the most populous nation until the end of the century. 

Here's a more detailed breakdown of the projections:

- **Current Population:**
    
    India's population is currently estimated at around 1.45 billion. 
    
- **Peak Population:**
    
    The United Nations projects that India's population will peak at around 1.7 billion in the early 2060s, specifically around 2062. 
    

- **Post-Peak Decline:**
    
    After reaching its peak, India's population is expected to start declining, though the country will still remain the world's most populous nation until the end of the century. 
    

- **2100 Projections:**
    
    Some estimates project India's population to decline to around 1.5 billion by 2100. 
    

- **Factors Influencing Projections:**
    
    These projections are based on data on fertility, mortality, and other demographic indicators. 
    

- **India's population growth on a downward trajectory**
    
    The average annual exponential growth of India's population is on a downward trajectory and is expected to decline from 2.20 per cent in 1971 to 1 per cent in 2024, placing the national population in the range of 138-142 crore in 2024, according to SBI's economic research department (ERD).
