### Purpose

- Increase **blood flow** 🩸
    
- Improve **range of motion**
    
- Reduce risk of **strain** (common with repetitive fretting/picking)
    

---

## 🔧 Short routine (3–5 minutes)

### 1) Wrist circles (30–60 sec)

- Arms relaxed
    
- Rotate wrists slowly both directions
    
- Keep movement controlled (not loose/floppy)
    

---

### 2) Flexor stretch (20–30 sec each side)

- Arm straight, palm facing **down**
    
- Use other hand to gently pull fingers **toward you**
    
- Stretch felt on inner forearm
    

---

### 3) Extensor stretch (20–30 sec each side)

- Arm straight, palm facing **up**
    
- Pull fingers **downward**
    
- Stretch felt on outer forearm
    

---

### 4) Prayer stretch (20–30 sec)

- Palms together in front of chest
    
- Lower hands slowly while keeping palms pressed
    
- Stop before discomfort
    

---

### 5) Finger tendon glides (30–45 sec)

Sequence:

- Open hand → Hook fist → Full fist → Straight fist → Open
    
- Move smoothly, no force
    

---

## ⚠️ Constraints

- No sharp pain; only mild stretch tension
    
- Avoid bouncing (static holds only)
    
- Keep shoulders relaxed
    
- If you feel tingling/numbness, stop
    

---

## 🎸 Transition into playing

After stretching:

- Do **very slow chromatic warmups (1–2–3–4)** for 2–3 minutes
    
- Start at low pressure, gradually increase
    

---

## 🧠 Practical note

Stretching alone isn’t sufficient—**technique matters more**:

- Thumb relaxed behind neck
    
- Minimal pressure on fretting
    
- Neutral wrist (avoid extreme bends)
